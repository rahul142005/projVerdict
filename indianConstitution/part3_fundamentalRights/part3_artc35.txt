Exclusive Power of Parliament: Only Parliament can make laws on:

Matters related to specific provisions of this Constitution, including laws about certain rights and their enforcement.
Punishments for offenses defined in this Part of the Constitution.
Existing Laws: Any laws in force before the Constitution began, related to these matters or punishments, will remain valid until Parliament changes or repeals them.

Explanation: "Law in force" means any law that was effective before the Constitution came into effect, as explained in Article 372.
