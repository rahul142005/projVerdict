Laws about land and property acquisition or management are protected from being challenged as violating fundamental rights. This includes:

Acquiring land or modifying property rights.
Taking over property management for public or proper management purposes.
Merging corporations.
Changing rights related to managing agents or mineral agreements.
State laws on these matters must be approved by the President to be valid. For land used for personal cultivation, compensation must be at least the market value.

Definitions:

Estate includes various types of land tenures and agricultural land.
Rights cover landholder privileges and rights related to land revenue.
