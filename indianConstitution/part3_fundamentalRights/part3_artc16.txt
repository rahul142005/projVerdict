Everyone should have equal opportunities for employment or appointment to any government job.

No citizen can be denied or treated unfairly for a government job based on religion, race, caste, sex, ancestry, place of birth, or residence.

Parliament can make laws requiring a certain period of residence in a state or Union territory before applying for some government jobs.

The government can reserve jobs or positions for backward classes that are not well-represented in government services.

4A. The government can also reserve promotions for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes if they are not adequately represented in government services.

4B. If there are reserved vacancies that are not filled in a given year, they can be treated as separate vacancies for future years and will not count towards the annual reservation limit of 50%.

This article does not affect laws that require specific religions or denominations for certain roles in religious or denominational institutions.
