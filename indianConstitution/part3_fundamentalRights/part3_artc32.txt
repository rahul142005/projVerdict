Right to Approach the Supreme Court: Citizens have the guaranteed right to move the Supreme Court to enforce their fundamental rights.

Powers of the Supreme Court: The Supreme Court can issue orders or writs (such as habeas corpus, mandamus, prohibition, quo warranto, and certiorari) to enforce these rights.

Empowerment of Other Courts: Parliament can authorize other courts to exercise some or all of the powers of the Supreme Court for enforcing fundamental rights, within their jurisdiction.

Suspension of Rights: The right to move the Supreme Court cannot be suspended except as allowed by the Constitution.
