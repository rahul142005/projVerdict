Everyone has the right to follow, practice, and share their religion, as long as it doesn't disturb public order, morality, or health.

The government can make laws:

(a) To manage or limit non-religious activities connected with religion, like economic or political activities.
(b) To promote social welfare and reform, and to allow all Hindus (including Sikhs, Jains, and Buddhists) access to Hindu religious institutions.
Explanations:

Wearing and carrying kirpans is part of the Sikh religion.
References to Hindus also include Sikhs, Jains, and Buddhists, and Hindu religious institutions include those open to these groups.
