The government cannot discriminate against any citizen based on religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth.

No citizen can be treated unfairly or be restricted in:

(a) Accessing shops, public restaurants, hotels, or places of entertainment, or
(b) Using public facilities like wells, tanks, bathing places, roads, or other public areas funded by the government.
The government can make special provisions for women and children.

The government can also make special provisions to help socially and educationally backward classes, Scheduled Castes, and Scheduled Tribes.

The government can create special laws to help these backward classes, Scheduled Castes, and Scheduled Tribes in areas like education, including admission to schools and colleges (except minority institutions).
