Any laws in India that were in effect before this Constitution started and are not in line with this Constitution are invalid to the extent they don't match.

The government cannot make any laws that reduce or take away the rights given by this Constitution. Any such law that does this will be invalid.

In this article:

(a) "Law" means any ordinance, order, rule, regulation, or custom that has legal power in India.
(b) "Laws in force" include any laws made before this Constitution started, even if they are not currently in effect or in certain areas.
This article does not apply to changes made to the Constitution under Article 368.
