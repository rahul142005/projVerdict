Even if someone doesn't meet the criteria in Article 5, a person who moved to India from what is now Pakistan will be considered a citizen if:

(a) They, their parents, or their grandparents were born in India (as defined by the Government of India Act, 1935), and
(b) Either:
(i) They moved to India before July 19, 1948, and have lived in India since moving, or
(ii) They moved to India on or after July 19, 1948, and were registered as an Indian citizen by a special officer before the Constitution started. To be registered, they must have lived in India for at least six months right before applying.
